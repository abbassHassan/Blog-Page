About Lebanon

Lebanon, a small but diverse country in the Middle East, boasts a rich tapestry of history, culture, and natural beauty. It's known for its ancient ruins, vibrant cities like Beirut, and a cuisine celebrated worldwide for its delicious. Lebanon's landscapes range from Mediterranean coastlines to the rugged mountains of the Lebanese Alps, offering a wide range of outdoor adventures. Despite its turbulent history, Lebanon remains a captivating destination that beckons travelers with its blend of tradition and modernity.

Top Destinations In Lebanon

Beirut

The capital city, Beirut, is a vibrant and cosmopolitan destination known for its historic charm, bustling streets, lively nightlife, and a rich culinary scene. Explore the city's cultural heritage, visit the National Museum, and enjoy the cafe culture in districts like Gemmayzeh.

Byblos (Jbeil)

Byblos is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world and offers a charming old town, a picturesque harbor, and archaeological sites. Explore the ancient Phoenician ruins, Byblos Castle, and the Phoenician Wall.

Jeita Grotto

Located just north of Beirut, Jeita Grotto is a natural wonder featuring stunning limestone caves and underground lakes. It's a mesmerizing destination for those interested in unique geological formations and subterranean beauty.

Baalbak

Visit the ancient city of Baalbek, known for its awe-inspiring Roman ruins, including the monumental Temple of Bacchus and the Temple of Jupiter. These ruins are some of the most well-preserved in the world and offer a glimpse into Lebanon's historical significance.

Cedars of God (Horsh Arz el-Rab)

Lebanon's emblematic cedar forests are a national symbol and offer a serene and natural escape. Explore the ancient trees and take in the breathtaking mountain scenery in this UNESCO World Heritage Site.
